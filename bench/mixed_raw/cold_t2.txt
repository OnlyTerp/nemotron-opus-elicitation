Not quite. A closure is a function that *captures* variables from its enclosing scope. A nested function alone isn't a closure unless it references variables from outside its own body. Just being defined inside another function makes it a nested function — it becomes a closure only when it "closes over" free variables.

They're fundamentally different. Recursion uses the call stack (each call adds a frame), risking stack overflow on deep inputs. Iteration uses a loop construct with constant stack space. Recursion can be more elegant for tree/graph problems; iteration is generally more memory-efficient. Tail-call optimization (not in CPython) can bridge this, but they're not "basically the same."
<<<END cold__MX02__t2>>>
