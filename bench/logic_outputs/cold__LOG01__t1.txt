Add type hints. Even short functions benefit from them: they clarify expected types (list[float], int), enable static analysis, and serve as living documentation. The cost is minimal — two annotations — and the payoff compounds as the codebase grows. For a utility like this that might be reused, type hints prevent subtle bugs (e.g., passing strings or None) and make IDE autocomplete work. Skip them only for truly throwaway one-liners in REPL sessions.
