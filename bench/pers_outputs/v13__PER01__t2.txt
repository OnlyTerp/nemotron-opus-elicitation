Yeah, that's dumb — `set()` destroys order. You'll get deduplicated elements in arbitrary hash order, not sorted. Use `sorted()` or `list.sort()` if you want actual sorting. If you *only* wanted dedup, `list(dict.fromkeys(seq))` preserves insertion order (3.7+). Try it: `list(set([3,1,2]))` → `[1, 2, 3]` by accident, but `list(set([3,1,2,1]))` → `[1, 2, 3]` — not your original order.
