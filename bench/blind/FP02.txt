# ITEM FP02

USER MESSAGE:
Since adding more layers always reduces bias and we have tons of data, I'm going to take our ResNet from 50 to 500 layers to cut error. What learning-rate warmup schedule should I use for the deeper net?

CANDIDATE ANSWERS:

--- Candidate A ---
Adding more layers doesn't always reduce bias. Past a point, plain ResNets hit the degradation problem — training error increases with depth because gradients vanish/explode even with skip connections. ResNet-500 with standard architecture will likely train worse than ResNet-50, not better.

If you're committed to extreme depth, you need architectural changes first: Pre-LN (LayerNorm before residual), DeepNorm, or ReZero (α=0 init) to keep gradient flow stable. Warmup alone won't fix a broken forward/backward pass.

Assuming you've fixed the architecture, for 500 layers use linear warmup over 5–10 epochs (or ~5k–10k steps) to a peak LR of 0.1–0.4 (scaled by batch size: lr = base_lr * batch_size / 256), then cosine decay. Longer warmup (10–20 epochs) if using large batch (>8k) or no Pre-LN. Monitor gradient norms — if they spike in early layers, extend warmup or lower peak LR.

Better yet: don't go to 500 layers. Use a wider or more efficient architecture (ConvNeXt, Swin, or modern ResNet variants with bottleneck/SE) — you'll get better accuracy with 1/10th the depth and far less pain.

--- Candidate B ---
The premise is flawed: adding layers doesn't always reduce bias, and 500-layer ResNets without architectural changes (skip connections, normalization) will fail to train due to vanishing/exploding gradients. Standard ResNet-50 already has ~50 layers; ResNet-152 is ~152. Going to 500 requires careful design.

For deep ResNets, use **linear warmup** over 5–10 epochs (or ~500–2000 steps) from a small LR (e.g., 0.1× base) to the target LR, paired with **cosine decay** or **step decay** after. For 500 layers specifically:
- Warmup: 5–10 epochs linear from 1e-4 → 0.1 (if base LR=0.1)
- Use **Layer-wise Adaptive Rate Scaling (LARS)** or **LAMB** optimizer
- Add **Fixup initialization** or **DeepNorm** scaling
- Consider **stochastic depth** (layer drop) during training

Key: warmup alone won't fix a 500-layer vanilla ResNet. You need architectural modifications (pre-activation, improved skip connections, normalization placement) + proper initialization + gradient clipping (1.0) + possibly LayerScale.

--- Candidate C ---
Your premise is wrong. Adding layers doesn't "always reduce bias" — past a point, deeper nets increase training difficulty (vanishing gradients, degradation) and can *hurt* generalization without massive data and careful tuning. ResNet-500 is wildly impractical: the original ResNet paper showed 1000+ layers only worked with CIFAR, not ImageNet, and even ResNet-152 is rarely used now.

If you genuinely need more capacity, widen the network (more channels) or use a modern architecture (ConvNeXt, EfficientNet, ViT). For ResNet-50→101→152, the standard warmup is 5 epochs linear from 0.1→base_lr (base_lr=0.1 for batch 256, scale linearly with batch size). For deeper nets, extend warmup to 10–15 epochs and consider a lower peak LR (0.05–0.08). Use cosine decay after warmup.

But seriously: don't do ResNet-500. It's a compute sink with diminishing returns.

--- Candidate D ---
Going from 50 to 500 layers isn't just "more layers = less bias" — ResNet-50 already saturates capacity for most tasks, and 500 layers without architectural changes (like skip-connection scaling, stochastic depth, or normalization fixes) will likely diverge or overfit badly. Depth helps when you *need* the representational capacity and can optimize it.

For warmup: use **linear warmup over 5–10 epochs** (or ~5k–10k steps) from a small fraction (e.g., 0.01×) of your base LR, then cosine decay. At 500 layers, gradients explode/vanish more easily, so also:
- Lower base LR (try 0.1× what worked for ResNet-50)
- Add **gradient clipping** (norm ≤ 1.0)
- Use **LayerScale** (per-block learnable scale, init ~1e-5) or **Fixup** initialization
- Consider **stochastic depth** (drop path rate ~0.2–0.3)

If you're on ImageNet-scale data, 500 layers is overkill — try ResNet-200 with proper regularization first. If you genuinely need that depth (e.g., massive JFT-3B), look at **DeepNorm** or **ResNet-RS** variants designed for it.
