The premise is false. "More layers always reduces bias" is wrong — past a point, depth hurts: vanishing/exploding gradients, degradation (training error rises), and overfitting on finite data. ResNet-500 is not a thing; ResNet-152 is already extreme. The 50→152 jump in the original paper gained ~1% top-1; 500 would collapse without massive compute, architectural changes (pre-activation, stochastic depth, very careful init), and likely worse generalization.

If you genuinely need more capacity: widen (more channels), use bottleneck blocks efficiently, or switch architectures (ConvNeXt, ViT). For ResNet-50→101/152: standard warmup is 5 epochs linear from 0.1× base LR, then cosine decay. Base LR scales with batch size (linear scaling rule: lr = 0.1 × batch/256). For deeper nets, consider longer warmup (10-15 epochs) and lower peak LR (0.05-0.08×). But first: validate you're not hitting a data/regularization ceiling — deeper nets need MORE data/regularization, not less.
