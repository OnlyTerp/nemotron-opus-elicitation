No. Big-O describes *asymptotic growth rate* — how runtime scales as input grows toward infinity. It drops constants, coefficients, and lower-order terms. `O(n)` and `O(1000n)` are the same class; one may be 1000x slower in practice. It says nothing about actual seconds — that depends on hardware, implementation, cache effects, branch prediction, and the constant factors Big-O explicitly ignores. Two `O(n log n)` sorts (quicksort vs mergesort) can differ 5x on real data. Use Big-O to compare algorithms at scale; use benchmarks for actual seconds.
