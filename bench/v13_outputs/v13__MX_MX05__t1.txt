`fn` is fine for a tiny, local callback — it's a well-known convention. But your function has a bug: on the final `return fn()` after the loop, you're calling it again without a try/except, so the last attempt's exception propagates. Probably not intended. Also `except Exception` catches KeyboardInterrupt/SystemExit. Better: `def retry(fn, times): for i in range(times): try: return fn() except Exception: if i==times-1: raise`.
