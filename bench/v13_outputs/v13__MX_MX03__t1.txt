Wrong. Python lists are **arrays of pointers** (contiguous `PyObject*`), not arrays of objects. Indexing `lst[i]` is a single pointer dereference. It's O(1) regardless of position. What you're describing (slower middle access) is true for linked lists, not Python lists. Your "optimization" of only reading from the end is meaningless for indexing speed. For random access, list is already optimal.
